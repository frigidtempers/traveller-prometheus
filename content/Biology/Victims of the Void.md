## Overview

The designation **Victims of the Void** refers to organisms altered through prolonged exposure to Void phenomena, most commonly active **Void Monoliths** and the parasitic condition known as **Monolithic Enteroneural Parasitosis (MEP)**. These entities exhibit profound physiological and neurological changes that cannot be explained through conventional xenobiology.

Although many retain traces of their original anatomy, all confirmed Victims display aggressive behavior, extensive biological restructuring, and complete loss of higher cognitive function. Standard quarantine protocols classify all confirmed infections as immediately hazardous. 

---

## Skinsloth

Among the earliest documented stages of advanced Void corruption, Skinsloths outwardly resemble heavily mutilated humans. Despite their shambling movement, they retain enough coordination to wield crude tools and improvised weapons.

- **Hits:** 7–11
- **Speed:** 5m
- **Skills:** Melee 0, Gun Combat 0, Athletics (Endurance) 0
- **Attacks:** Clumsy Swing (1D), Bite (1D-1)
- **Traits:** Strange, Diseased, Tool-User (Crude), Ornery
- **Special:** **Parasite Escape** — Upon death, a Gutworm emerges adjacent to the corpse on the following round.

---

## Splitborn

Splitborn are heavily mutated organisms capable of physically dividing into multiple independent lifeforms after sustaining sufficient trauma. This behavior dramatically increases the threat posed by even a single specimen.

- **Hits:** 14–18
- **Speed:** 5m
- **Skills:** Athletics (Dexterity) 1, Melee (Natural) 1
- **Attacks:** Bone Glaive (2D, AP 1), Chittering Lunge (1D+2)
- **Traits:** Armor (+2), Strange, Ornery
- **Special:** **Split** — At half Hits, divides into two Crawlers. Upon death, ruptures into **1D Gutworms**.

## Bombardier

Bombardiers develop unstable chemical organs capable of projecting corrosive compounds over considerable distances. Destruction of these organs results in an explosive release of fire and acid.

- **Hits:** 8–13
- **Speed:** 6m
- **Skills:** Melee (Natural) 1, Athletics (Endurance) 1, Recon 0
- **Attacks:** Caustic Jet (10m), Bludgeon (D3)
- **Traits:** Strange, Ornery, Diseased
- **Special:** **Reactive Sacs** — Detonates upon death, dealing **3D Blast 4** and coating survivors in corrosive acid.

---

## Gutworm

Gutworms are parasitic organisms responsible for transmitting **Monolithic Enteroneural Parasitosis**. Individually they present only a minor physical threat, but infection frequently proves far more dangerous than the initial attack.

- **Hits:** 2
- **Speed:** 6m
- **Attacks:** Burrow Bite (1D-2)
- **Traits:** Strange, Tiny (-3)
- **Special:** Successful attacks may transmit **MEP**. 

---

## Gutworm Swarm

When numerous Gutworms gather, they behave as a single dispersive organism capable of rapidly overwhelming unprotected victims.

- **Hits:** 6–9
- **Speed:** 6m
- **Attacks:** Swarm Worry (1D)
- **Traits:** Dispersed, Strange, Vulnerable (Fire)
- **Special:** Ignores ordinary clothing; sealed armor provides normal protection.

## Monolithic Enteroneural Parasitosis (MEP)

Commonly referred to as **Gutworming**, MEP is a progressive parasitic infection originating from exposure to Gutworms or active Void Monoliths. Infection begins within the digestive tract before spreading along the enteric nervous system toward the brain, ultimately resulting in irreversible neurological collapse.

### Disease Progression

| Stage | Effects |
|-------|---------|
| **Stage I — Prodrome** | Nausea, abdominal pain, increased hunger, **DM-1 Recon** and **Initiative**. |
| **Stage II — Dysautonomia** | Tremors, cognitive disruption, **DM-1 INT** checks. |
| **Stage III — Enteroneural Overwrite** | Compulsions, hallucinations, loss of voluntary control. |
| **Stage IV — Puppet State** | Complete neurological collapse. Victim eventually transforms into an early-stage Void organism. |

Fire, complete quarantine, and immediate surgical intervention remain the only documented methods of reliably preventing terminal progression once infection has occurred.
