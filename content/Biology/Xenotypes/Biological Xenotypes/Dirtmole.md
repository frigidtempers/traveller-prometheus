## Overview

The **Dirtmole** is a biological xenotype engineered for permanent subterranean habitation. Adapted for life beneath the surface of mining worlds and underground arcologies, Dirtmoles possess enhanced physical endurance, exceptional spatial awareness in confined environments, and a natural tolerance for darkness.

Although originally developed as industrial laborers, Dirtmoles are now found in every sector of human civilization. Their expertise in mining, tunneling, and underground construction has made them indispensable wherever valuable resources lie beneath the surface.

---

## Biology

Dirtmoles possess pale gray skin, reduced pigmentation, and enlarged pupils adapted for dimly lit environments. Their eyesight performs exceptionally well underground but becomes impaired in bright sunlight, making prolonged surface work uncomfortable without protective equipment.

Generations of genetic engineering have produced a compact, resilient physique well suited to physically demanding labor. Dirtmoles generally exhibit greater endurance than baseliners and are capable of working for extended periods in confined environments where other xenotypes struggle.

---

## Society

Mining remains the profession most closely associated with Dirtmoles, though their expertise extends to excavation, heavy industry, tunnel construction, and geological surveying.

Many Dirtmole communities maintain strong traditions centered around cooperation and craftsmanship. Whether employed by Corporate Houses, independent mining concerns, or frontier settlements, they are widely respected for their technical knowledge and willingness to work in environments few others find tolerable.

---

## Presence on Prometheus

Dirtmoles are commonly encountered throughout Prometheus wherever mining operations have been established.

Many work within the planet's industrial settlements, while others operate as independent prospectors searching the frontier for valuable mineral deposits. Tribal communities occasionally include Dirtmoles whose expertise in cave systems and underground travel has proven invaluable for generations.
