## Overview

The **Baseliner** is the original human xenotype from which all other biological xenotypes are derived. Representing the natural human genome prior to widespread genetic engineering, baseliners remain one of the most widely distributed human populations throughout inhabited space.

Although many civilizations employ specialized xenotypes for particular occupations or environments, baseliners continue to serve in every aspect of human society. Their genetic versatility, adaptability, and compatibility with xenogenetic modification have ensured their continued presence across worlds of every classification.

---

## Biology

Baseliners possess no inheritable genetic modifications beyond the natural variation found within the human species.

Unlike engineered xenotypes, baseliners are not optimized for a specific environment or occupation. Their balanced physiology allows them to perform effectively across a broad range of conditions without exhibiting the specialized advantages or limitations associated with purpose-built genomes.

Because the baseliner genome serves as the foundation for nearly all modern xenogenetic engineering, it remains the standard reference used in medical, biological, and genetic research throughout human civilization.

---

## Society

Baseliners occupy every level of human civilization, from isolated frontier settlements to the most technologically advanced worlds.

Their absence of engineered specialization allows them to integrate easily into populations composed of multiple xenotypes. As a result, baseliners frequently serve alongside genetically engineered humans in government, industry, scientific research, agriculture, military service, and countless other professions.

Although some societies favor specialized xenotypes for particular roles, baseliners remain the most broadly represented human population throughout the known galaxy.

---

## Presence on Prometheus

Baseliners are found throughout Prometheus and are represented within nearly every major faction operating on the planet.

Imperial settlements, independent outlander communities, tribal societies, pirate organizations, and isolated homesteads all maintain significant baseliner populations. Their widespread distribution reflects successive waves of colonization, migration, and natural population growth over many centuries.
