## Overview

The **Genie** is a biological xenotype engineered for scientific, technical, and intellectual pursuits. Developed to excel in research, engineering, medicine, and other knowledge-intensive professions, Genies possess exceptional cognitive abilities at the expense of physical resilience and social aptitude.

Although comparatively uncommon among the general population, Genies are widely employed wherever advanced technical expertise is required. Their contributions have shaped countless fields, from xenogenetics and medicine to spacecraft design and industrial engineering.

---

## Biology

Genies exhibit enhanced neural development and accelerated cognitive processing, allowing them to analyze complex problems with remarkable speed and precision. This heightened intelligence is accompanied by a noticeably frail physique, leaving most Genies less capable of prolonged physical exertion than other xenotypes.

Many also display reserved or socially awkward behavior. While capable communicators when discussing subjects within their expertise, Genies often struggle with interpersonal interaction outside of professional environments.

---

## Society

Genies are commonly employed as researchers, physicians, engineers, technicians, and educators. Many spend their entire careers within laboratories, research institutes, orbital facilities, or industrial complexes where their specialized knowledge is highly valued.

Although respected for their intellect, Genies are often perceived as distant or eccentric by other xenotypes. Many prefer solving problems to navigating social expectations, earning a reputation for practicality, curiosity, and unwavering focus.

---

## Presence on Prometheus

Genies remain relatively uncommon on Prometheus compared to baseliners and other frontier xenotypes.

Those who settle on the planet are typically employed by Corporate Houses, scientific expeditions, industrial operations, or orbital facilities requiring advanced technical expertise. Independent Genies are frequently encountered as engineers, physicians, or researchers supporting isolated settlements where specialized knowledge is scarce.
