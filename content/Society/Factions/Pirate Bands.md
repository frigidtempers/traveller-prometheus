## Overview

**Pirate Band** is a broad classification applied to independent criminal organizations operating beyond the authority of established governments. While their methods and motivations differ considerably, most sustain themselves through raiding, smuggling, extortion, piracy, and black-market commerce.

Unlike the Shattered Imperium or the Corporate Houses, Pirate Bands are rarely long-lived institutions. Most exist only as long as their leadership remains intact, with successful groups either collapsing through internal conflict or evolving into larger criminal organizations.

Pirate Bands are generally categorized by the scale of their operations rather than any shared ideology.

---

## Pirate Gangs

Pirate Gangs are localized criminal organizations operating from abandoned settlements, ruined industrial facilities, caves, or hidden encampments. Most lack access to spacecraft, relying instead upon ground raids, extortion, smuggling, and theft to sustain themselves.

Although individually limited in scope, Pirate Gangs represent the most common form of organized crime encountered throughout the Rim.

### Example: The Rustclads

The **Rustclads** are a loose collection of scrap cartels scattered throughout the Rim. Operating from abandoned mines, derelict factories, and forgotten settlements, they salvage, repair, and weaponize ancient machinery before selling it through black-market networks.

Individual Rustclad cartels frequently feud over territory and salvage rights, but all adhere to a simple philosophy:

> *"If you can't fix it, it isn't yours."*

---

## Pirate Crews

A Pirate Crew is centered around a single interstellar vessel commanded by a captain and supported by a relatively small crew. Possessing a ship allows these organizations to conduct orbital raids, intercept merchant traffic, smuggle contraband, and relocate between systems before local authorities can respond.

For most pirates, acquiring a ship marks the transition from local criminals to true space pirates.

### Example: The Darwin Collective

Despite its name, the **Darwin Collective** operates more like a pirate crew than a conventional research organization. Based aboard the laboratory ship *ISS Tortuga*, the Collective conducts illegal biomedical experiments through kidnapping, forced augmentation, and organ harvesting across frontier worlds.

Rather than occupying territory, the Collective appears without warning, abducts suitable subjects, and disappears before organized resistance can be mounted.

---

## Pirate Syndicates

Pirate Syndicates represent the largest criminal organizations commonly encountered beyond civilized space.

Rather than relying upon a single vessel, Syndicates command small fleets capable of sustaining operations across multiple star systems. Hidden ports, supply caches, intelligence networks, and subordinate captains allow them to maintain a persistent presence along frontier trade routes.

Even so, Pirate Syndicates remain insignificant compared to the military capabilities of the Shattered Imperium or the Corporate Houses, surviving through mobility, secrecy, and opportunism rather than direct confrontation.

### Example: The Boa-Teimar Fleets

The **Boa-Teimar Fleets** are among the best-known pirate syndicates operating throughout the frontier. Descended from isolated colony survivors, the organization commands several heavily modified vessels operating from the derelict colony ark known as **The Wound Star**.

The Syndicate specializes in commerce raiding, smuggling, and attacks against poorly defended settlements and corporate shipping. While feared by merchants, some frontier communities view the Boa-Teimar captains as reluctant outlaws who target institutions they believe abandoned humanity's forgotten colonies.
