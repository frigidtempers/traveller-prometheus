## Overview

**House Xiaomu** is one of the five major Corporate Houses and the foremost authority in medicine, biotechnology, and genetic science throughout known space. Through an extensive network of hospitals, pharmaceutical laboratories, prosthetics manufacturers, biomedical research institutes, and xenogenetics firms, House Xiaomu provides much of the medical infrastructure upon which human civilization depends.

Although best known through its flagship corporation, **Meditech**, House Xiaomu's influence extends far beyond healthcare. Its affiliated organizations are responsible for advancements in regenerative medicine, artificial organs, cybernetic prosthetics, xenogerm development, and countless other fields that have transformed modern medicine.

---

## History

House Xiaomu traces its origins to a coalition of medical research foundations and pharmaceutical institutions established during humanity's early expansion beyond Old Earth. As isolated colonies confronted unfamiliar diseases, environmental hazards, and the challenges of long-duration interstellar travel, cooperation between these organizations became increasingly essential.

Over centuries, countless laboratories, hospitals, and biomedical companies consolidated beneath House Xiaomu, creating one of the oldest and most influential medical institutions in human history. Today, its subsidiaries provide healthcare and biomedical research across every level of civilization, from glitterworld hospitals to frontier field clinics.

---

## Organization

House Xiaomu oversees a decentralized network of independently managed medical organizations, research institutes, pharmaceutical manufacturers, and biotechnology firms.

Individual subsidiaries maintain considerable autonomy in both research and administration while operating under House-established standards governing medical ethics, quality assurance, and scientific collaboration. This structure allows discoveries made on one world to gradually disseminate throughout the House's broader network despite the immense distances separating inhabited systems.

---

## Culture

House Xiaomu regards medicine as a responsibility shared across generations.

Scientific progress is measured not only through discovery, but through its successful application to improve the quality of human life. Employees are encouraged to preserve knowledge, document their work thoroughly, and regard every advancement as a foundation upon which future generations will continue to build.

Among House personnel, a common saying persists:

> *"Every life preserved strengthens humanity."*

## Notable Holdings

The following organizations represent only a small portion of House Xiaomu's commercial portfolio.

- **Meditech** — Hospitals, clinical services, and biomedical research.
- **Lazarus Prosthetics** — Cybernetic limbs, synthetic organs, and rehabilitative medicine.
- **Vitae Pharmaceuticals** — Pharmaceutical research and industrial medicine.
- **Genesis Xenogenics** — Xenogerm development, gene therapy, and genetic engineering.
- **Apex Medical Solutions** — Emergency medicine, expeditionary healthcare, and colonial medical infrastructure.

---

## Relations

House Xiaomu maintains close relationships with each of the major Corporate Houses.

Its hospitals rely upon Belmar-manufactured medical equipment, Amora's transportation network delivers pharmaceutical supplies across inhabited space, Helix collaborates extensively in nutrition and environmental health, and House Gol supports medical education and public health initiatives through its communications networks.

Although commercially competitive, House Xiaomu frequently participates in cooperative research projects benefiting the wider human population.

---

## Presence on Prometheus

House Xiaomu maintains hospitals, research facilities, pharmaceutical distribution centers, and biomedical laboratories throughout Prometheus.

Much of the Rimworld's advanced medical care is provided through Meditech facilities or affiliated organizations operating under House Xiaomu's authority. Even settlements beyond the reach of permanent hospitals often rely upon medical equipment, pharmaceuticals, and prosthetics manufactured by one of the House's many subsidiaries.
