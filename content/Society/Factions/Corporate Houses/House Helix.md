## Overview

**House Helix** is one of the five major Corporate Houses and humanity's foremost authority on agriculture, ecological engineering, and planetary sustainability. Through an immense network of agricultural firms, terraforming contractors, hydroponics manufacturers, water reclamation companies, and environmental consultancies, House Helix provides the essential resources required to establish and sustain human life across the stars.

While often associated with food production, the House's responsibilities extend far beyond farming. From atmospheric processors and irrigation systems to soil restoration and ecological management, House Helix specializes in transforming hostile environments into viable homes for human settlement.

---

## History

House Helix originated during humanity's earliest waves of extrasolar colonization, when newly founded settlements required reliable food production and environmental stabilization to survive. Numerous agricultural cooperatives and colonial development firms gradually united beneath a single commercial dynasty, laying the foundation for what would become one of humanity's largest Corporate Houses.

As interstellar civilization expanded, House Helix diversified into nearly every aspect of planetary development. Today, its affiliated corporations oversee projects ranging from hydroponic megafarms and genetic crop research to atmospheric restoration and long-term terraforming initiatives.

---

## Organization

House Helix governs a decentralized network of corporations specializing in agriculture, environmental science, biotechnology, and colonial infrastructure.

Although each subsidiary operates independently, they share common agricultural standards, environmental regulations, and research initiatives established by the House. This structure enables local companies to adapt to the unique ecological conditions of individual worlds while benefiting from centuries of accumulated expertise.

---

## Culture

House Helix believes that civilization begins with self-sufficiency.

Employees are encouraged to think in generations rather than fiscal quarters. Successful projects are measured not by immediate profit, but by whether a settlement can remain productive decades or centuries after its founding.

Among House personnel, a common saying persists:

> *"No civilization takes root in barren soil."*

## Notable Holdings

The following organizations represent only a small portion of House Helix's commercial portfolio.

- **NuTerra Corp.** — Agricultural development and colonial sustainability.
- **Verdant Colonial Services** — Planetary settlement planning and environmental management.
- **Greenleaf Foods** — Industrial agriculture and food distribution.
- **Arbor Ecological Systems** — Water reclamation, atmospheric processing, and habitat restoration.
- **Evergreen Hydroponics** — Hydroponic infrastructure and controlled-environment agriculture.

---

## Relations

House Helix maintains extensive partnerships with every major Corporate House.

House Belmar manufactures much of the industrial equipment used in Helix facilities, while House Amora transports agricultural products and colonial supplies throughout inhabited space. House Xiaomu frequently collaborates with Helix in the fields of nutrition, public health, and xenobotanical research.

Although political influence has never been the House's primary objective, few governments can afford to ignore an institution responsible for feeding billions of people.

---

## Presence on Prometheus

House Helix maintains a significant presence across Prometheus through agricultural settlements, hydroponic facilities, seed banks, ecological research stations, and colonial development projects.

Many frontier communities rely upon Helix-designed farming equipment, genetically engineered crops, and environmental infrastructure to remain self-sufficient. Even settlements operating independently often utilize technology or agricultural practices developed by one of the House's many affiliated corporations.
