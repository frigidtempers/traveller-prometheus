## Overview

An **ideoligion** is a community united by shared religious, philosophical, or cultural beliefs rather than political authority or territorial control. Unlike governments, corporations, or settlements, ideoligions exist wherever their followers gather, often spanning multiple worlds and countless independent societies.

An ideoligion may exist entirely within a single village, become the dominant belief system of an industrial city, or spread across dozens of colonies through missionaries, pilgrims, and oral tradition. Political affiliation rarely determines religious identity, and followers of the same ideoligion may be found among the Shattered Imperium, Corporate Houses, independent settlements, Pirate Bands, and Tribal Communities alike.

While many ideoligions exist peacefully alongside one another, others actively compete for converts or seek to reshape society according to their beliefs.

---

## Characteristics

Ideoligions commonly define a community's understanding of:

- Spiritual belief
- Morality and ethics
- Ritual and tradition
- Leadership and social structure
- Humanity's relationship with technology and nature
- Death and remembrance

Many also establish places of worship, communal gathering halls, monasteries, pilgrimage routes, or missionary organizations that preserve and spread their teachings.

---

## Missionary Orders

Some ideoligions actively seek to spread their beliefs beyond the communities in which they originated.

Missionaries, scholars, and pilgrims travel between settlements carrying religious texts, cultural traditions, and philosophical teachings. While most rely upon peaceful outreach, others believe conversion is a moral obligation that justifies more forceful methods.

### Example: The Keepers of the Flame

Originating from a small frontier monastery, the **Keepers of the Flame** teach that civilization survives only so long as its knowledge is preserved. Their monasteries maintain libraries, archives, and schools throughout the Rim, welcoming travelers willing to contribute knowledge in exchange for shelter and education.

---

## Philosophical Communities

Not every ideoligion centers on supernatural belief.

Some are secular philosophies that encourage particular ways of living, governing, or understanding humanity's place within the universe. These communities frequently establish communes, educational institutions, or charitable organizations dedicated to practicing their ideals.

### Example: The Ascendant Path

Followers of the **Ascendant Path** believe humanity's greatest strength lies in continual self-improvement through education, discipline, and scientific understanding. Although decentralized, their academies and discussion circles can be found throughout many independent settlements.

---

## Militant Ideoligions

Some ideoligions maintain armed orders dedicated to defending their communities or enforcing religious doctrine.

These organizations range from disciplined warrior-monks protecting sacred sites to extremist sects willing to wage war in pursuit of ideological goals. Although uncommon, militant ideoligions have shaped the histories of countless frontier worlds through both conflict and cooperation.

### Example: The Children of the Machine

The **Children of the Machine** believe advanced technology represents humanity's highest achievement and that the preservation of ancient machines is a sacred duty. Their members frequently seek forgotten ruins, abandoned research facilities, and dormant mechanoid complexes in pursuit of relics they consider worthy of veneration.

---

## Presence on Prometheus

Prometheus is home to numerous ideoligions reflecting the Rimworld's remarkable cultural diversity.

Some remain confined to individual settlements or tribal communities, while others maintain followers across the planet through missionary work, pilgrimage, or shared cultural traditions. As populations migrate and new colonies are established, ideoligions continue to evolve alongside the societies that practice them.
