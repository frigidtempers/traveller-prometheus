Modern medicine ranges from improvised frontier first aid to sophisticated diagnostic equipment capable of identifying injuries, disease, and toxic exposure within seconds. Although advanced hospitals are commonplace on glitterworlds, most physicians operating throughout the Rim depend upon portable equipment that can be carried into the field.

---

## Neutroamine

*"A synthetic precursor chemical. Useless on its own, many drugs require neutroamine as an ingredient."*

Utilized in the manufacture of pharmaceuticals, both medicinal and recreational. Neutroamine also serves as an essential biochemical component in the maintenance and repair of mechanoids and other synthetic lifeforms.

**TL:** 8

**Mass:** 0.1 kg

**Cost:** Cr100 per vial

**Effect:** Neutroamine has no direct medical effect when administered to a patient. Instead, it is consumed as a crafting reagent in the production of pharmaceuticals, including antibiotics, stimulants, combat drugs, and other advanced medicines. Certain synthetic xenotypes and mechanoid repair procedures may also require Neutroamine at the Referee's discretion.

---

## Trauma Pack

*"A battlefield emergency kit designed to keep the dying alive just long enough to reach proper medical care."*

A compact satchel containing stimulants, coagulants, synthetic blood expanders, and emergency pharmaceuticals. Trauma Packs are standard issue among combat medics, emergency responders, and expeditionary crews.

**TL:** 8

**Mass:** 2 kg

**Cost:** Cr750

**Effect:** Requires a **Medic** check to administer. Restores **1D END** to a wounded patient, including those reduced to **0 END**, provided their final injury occurred within the last **1D rounds**.

The restored END lasts for **1D hours**, after which it is lost. A patient may only benefit from a Trauma Pack **once per day**.

---

## Medikit

*"Everything a physician needs until they reach a hospital."*

A portable collection of diagnostic scanners, surgical instruments, antibiotics, and emergency medical supplies. Higher-tech medikits incorporate increasingly sophisticated imaging equipment and biological sensors, dramatically improving a medic's ability to diagnose and stabilize patients in the field.

| TL | Cost | Effect |
|:--:|------:|--------|
| 8 | Cr1,000 | DM+0 to First Aid |
| 10 | Cr1,500 | DM+1 to First Aid |
| 12 | Cr5,000 | DM+2 to First Aid |
| 14 | Cr10,000 | DM+3 to First Aid |

All medikits allow **field surgery** to be performed. Surgery normally suffers **DM–2**, though the First Aid bonus provided by the medikit partially offsets this penalty.

---

## Personal Mediscanner

*"A physician's second opinion, worn on your belt."*

A compact diagnostic device that continuously monitors the wearer's vital signs, blood chemistry, hydration, body temperature, and other physiological indicators. It can rapidly identify illness, poisoning, exhaustion, and numerous medical conditions before symptoms become severe.

**TL:** 12

**Mass:** —

**Cost:** Cr1,000

**Effect:** Grants **DM+1** to **Medic** checks involving the monitored individual. 

---

## Portable Mediscanner

*"Designed for field medics treating patients they've never met."*

A larger diagnostic platform intended for emergency responders and expeditionary medical teams. Before use, the scanner requires **1D minutes** to calibrate itself to an unfamiliar patient, after which it provides comprehensive diagnostic information suitable for field treatment.

**TL:** 12

**Mass:** 1 kg

**Cost:** Cr2,000

**Effect:** After calibration, grants **DM+1** to **Medic** checks. Optional diagnostic packages for non-human species are commonly available.
