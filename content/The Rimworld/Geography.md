Prometheus is a terrestrial rimworld possessing a stable atmosphere, abundant liquid water, and extensive biological diversity. Conditions across the planet support permanent human habitation, although climate, terrain, and available resources vary considerably between regions.

Geographic isolation has played a significant role in the development of settlements throughout the planet's history. Mountain ranges, deserts, oceans, dense forests, and polar environments frequently separate communities by hundreds or thousands of kilometers, contributing to the formation of distinct cultures and independent political organizations.

---

## Climate

Prometheus supports a wide range of climatic conditions. Permanent settlements are established across temperate, tropical, arid, boreal, tundra, and polar regions where local resources permit long-term habitation.

Seasonal weather patterns vary considerably between latitudes and elevations. Droughts, cold seasons, flooding, and severe storms regularly influence agriculture, transportation, and settlement development.

---

## Biomes

The planet contains numerous ecological regions supporting diverse plant and animal life.

Environmental conditions influence local agriculture, available building materials, wildlife populations, and patterns of human settlement. Some regions remain largely uninhabited due to limited resources or environmental hazards.

---

## Natural Resources

Prometheus possesses significant mineral wealth, including extensive deposits of steel, uranium, plasteel, and other industrial resources.

Resource availability differs substantially between regions, encouraging trade while also contributing to territorial disputes between neighboring settlements and factions.

---

## Exploration

Large portions of Prometheus remain sparsely populated or undocumented.

Ancient ruins, abandoned settlements, industrial facilities, and other remnants of earlier civilizations are distributed throughout the planet. Many locations have yet to be systematically surveyed or excavated.

Geographic conditions, environmental hazards, and the absence of developed infrastructure continue to limit exploration in many regions.
