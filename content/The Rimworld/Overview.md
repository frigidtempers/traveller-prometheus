**Prometheus** is a rimworld located within the Orion Arm and serves as the default campaign setting for _Traveller: Prometheus_.

Like many rimworlds, Prometheus was settled during humanity's expansion into interstellar space and has since developed independently through successive periods of colonization, isolation, and resettlement. The planet supports numerous settlements with varying levels of technological development, ranging from tribal communities to industrial population centers.

Although capable of sustaining large populations, Prometheus remains politically fragmented. Independent settlements, regional governments, pirate bands, Imperial interests, and isolated homesteads occupy different regions of the planet, with no single authority exercising complete control.

Interstellar travel to and from Prometheus is infrequent due to the limitations of relativistic spaceflight. Orbital traffic consists primarily of commercial traders, independent spacecraft, Imperial vessels, and passing transport ships. Most inhabitants spend their entire lives without leaving the planet.

---

## Geography

Prometheus possesses a stable atmosphere, abundant surface water, and diverse ecological regions capable of supporting permanent human habitation.

The planet contains a wide range of climates and biomes, contributing to significant regional variation in settlement patterns, agriculture, wildlife, and available natural resources.

---

## History

Prometheus has undergone repeated periods of settlement, development, abandonment, and recovery throughout its recorded history.

Surviving evidence indicates the existence of multiple civilizations spanning different technological eras. Ancient ruins, abandoned industrial facilities, and derelict orbital infrastructure remain common across much of the planet.

For additional information, see [[History of Prometheus]].

---

## Current Conditions

Prometheus remains an inhabited and active rimworld.

Trade, migration, conflict, and exploration continue to reshape the political and economic landscape as settlements compete for territory, resources, and technological advantages.

Despite its isolation, the planet continues to receive occasional visitors from other inhabited systems, introducing new technologies, cultures, and opportunities alongside new conflicts.
