## Survey

Prometheus was identified as a suitable candidate for colonization during an early period of interstellar expansion.

Initial surveys documented a breathable atmosphere, abundant surface water, extensive mineral deposits, and a diverse native ecosystem. The planet was classified as capable of supporting long-term human habitation.

---

## Colonization

The earliest permanent settlements were established by colonists arriving aboard relativistic spacecraft.

Additional waves of settlement occurred over an extended period as independent expeditions, commercial ventures, religious groups, and governmental organizations established communities across the planet.

Many of these settlements developed independently, separated by geography, distance, and the significant travel times between inhabited star systems.

---

## Development

Archaeological evidence indicates that Prometheus supported settlements across multiple technological eras.

Surviving infrastructure includes abandoned industrial facilities, underground installations, orbital structures, transportation networks, and extensive urban ruins. Many of these sites remain only partially explored.

The relationship between individual settlements and civilizations remains uncertain due to incomplete historical records.

---

## Isolation

Interstellar travel to and from Prometheus became increasingly infrequent over time.

As contact with neighboring systems declined, surviving settlements became progressively more self-reliant. Technological development diverged between regions as local conditions, available resources, and cultural traditions influenced continued growth.

No surviving records identify a single event responsible for the planet's isolation.

---

## Present Day

Prometheus remains inhabited by numerous independent settlements representing a broad range of cultures, ideologies, and technological development.

Industrial communities, tribal societies, Imperial settlements, pirate enclaves, and isolated homesteads exist across the planet.

Ancient ruins and abandoned infrastructure from earlier periods remain common throughout the landscape, serving as valuable sources of materials, technology, and archaeological research.

No single faction exercises authority over the entire planet.
