Orbital infrastructure consists of artificial installations and spacecraft operating within the gravitational influence of Prometheus. Although substantially reduced from earlier periods of settlement, orbital facilities continue to support communication, trade, transportation, and limited industrial activity.

Most infrastructure presently in orbit dates to multiple periods of habitation and varies considerably in age, ownership, and operational condition.

---

## Communications

Orbital communications remain the primary means of establishing contact with passing spacecraft.

Most permanent settlements equipped with an Orbital Communications Console are capable of transmitting and receiving long-range communications through available orbital relays or directly with nearby vessels.

Communication beyond the local system remains limited by the speed of light.

---

## Trade

Commercial traffic consists primarily of independent merchant vessels transporting passengers, manufactured goods, industrial equipment, medicine, and raw materials between inhabited systems.

Orbital trade is infrequent compared to established interstellar trade routes closer to the galactic core. Many settlements may wait months or years before receiving an opportunity to conduct off-world commerce.

---

## Orbital Installations

Numerous artificial structures remain in orbit around Prometheus.

Known installations include communications satellites, trade platforms, research stations, navigation beacons, industrial facilities, and abandoned habitats.

Many installations are no longer operational, while others continue to function through automated maintenance systems or local oversight.

---

## Spacecraft

Civilian transports, commercial freighters, independent traders, Imperial vessels, and privately operated spacecraft periodically enter orbit around Prometheus.

Most vessels remain in orbit only long enough to exchange cargo, passengers, or information before continuing their voyage.

Permanent civilian spacecraft based within the system remain uncommon.

---

## Derelicts

Historical records indicate the loss of numerous spacecraft and orbital facilities throughout Prometheus' history.

Some remain in stable orbit, while others have fragmented into debris fields or undergone controlled deorbit procedures.

Derelict installations are frequently targeted by salvage expeditions due to the possibility of recovering advanced technology and industrial materials.

---

## Present Status

Orbital infrastructure continues to play a significant role in maintaining contact between Prometheus and the wider human civilization.

Although interstellar travel remains uncommon, orbital activity provides the primary connection between the planet and neighboring inhabited systems.
